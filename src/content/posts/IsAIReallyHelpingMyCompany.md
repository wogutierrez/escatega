It is easy to buy an AI tool. It is much harder to get real value from it. 

Too often, companies treat AI like a trophy. But its value doesn't come from owning the software—it comes entirely from what it helps your team accomplish. 

If an AI-generated summary goes unread, if a draft requires heavy editing, or if a faster workflow yields a weaker product, you aren't actually saving resources. You are just creating noise.

> **The real question isn't whether AI can do the work. It is whether the final work is better because of it.**

---

## The Trap: Activity vs. Benefit

When auditing AI, companies usually track easy metrics: licenses assigned, prompts entered, and active hours. 

But usage only shows activity. The real goal is **measurable benefit**. 

This gap is widespread. A recent MIT study on generative AI revealed that many enterprise deployments failed to improve intended outcomes, even when employee adoption was incredibly high. High adoption does not guarantee high performance. 

Leaders must ask:
1. Are our people using the tool?
2. Is that usage delivering value worthy of the investment?

---

## The Hidden Cost of Careless AI

Poorly managed AI introduces a subtle operational risk: it can cost more than it saves. 

Ten minutes saved by an employee using AI to draft a proposal can easily turn into forty minutes of painful correction by a manager. When AI is used without care—chosen strictly for speed or accepted blindly because of its confident tone—the costs do not disappear. They are simply hidden down the pipeline.

## The Two-Step Value Check

To ensure AI is pulling its weight, apply two quick checks to any workflow:

### 1. Before: Is the task suitable?
AI thrives on repetitive, well-defined, and time-consuming tasks. It struggles with work requiring deep strategic discernment, high-stakes empathy, or nuanced corporate communication. 

### 2. After: Is it delivering?
* **Did effort shift to review, or disappear?** If no one reviews the output, accountability is just delayed until a mistake hits a client.
* **Can you confidently endorse the result?** Speed means nothing if the underlying quality compromises your brand.
* **Was the saved time reinvested into core priorities?** The true ROI of AI is what your people accomplish with the hours they win back.
